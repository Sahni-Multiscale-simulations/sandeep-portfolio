About Me

Biography

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Science, specializing in the intersection of computational biology and machine learning. My research focuses on developing novel algorithms to analyze complex biological datasets and uncover meaningful patterns in disease progression and treatment response.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computational Biology at Tech University, where I developed innovative methods for high-throughput genomic data analysis. My work has been published in top-tier journals and presented at international conferences.

Research Interests

Machine Learning in Biomedicine

Developing deep learning models for disease prediction and personalized treatment recommendations using multi-omics data.

Genomic Data Analysis

Creating computational pipelines for large-scale genomic and transcriptomic data processing and interpretation.

Precision Medicine

Integrating clinical and molecular data to develop patient-specific therapeutic strategies for complex diseases.

Selected Publications

Deep learning approaches for cancer subtype classification

Researcher J., Colleague A., Senior P. (2023) Nature Computational Science

We present a novel deep neural network architecture that accurately classifies cancer subtypes using multi-modal data including gene expression, methylation, and clinical features.

Integrative analysis of single-cell RNA-seq datasets

Researcher J., Mentor K., Team L. (2022) Cell Systems

A computational framework for batch correction and integration of heterogeneous single-cell RNA sequencing datasets enabling large-scale comparative analyses.

Network-based drug repurposing for rare diseases

Collaborator M., Researcher J., et al. (2021) PLOS Computational Biology

We developed a network medicine approach that identifies potential therapeutic candidates for rare genetic disorders by analyzing protein-protein interaction networks.
